Basketball Rules

Basketball is played by two teams of five, aiming to score by shooting a ball through a 10-foot hoop. The team with the most points after four quarters (or sometimes 2 halves depending) wins, with 2 or 3 points awarded per shot based on distance from the basket. Players move the ball by dribbling or passing, with key violations including traveling, double dribbling, and 3-second violations.

Types of violations in basketball:

  1. Traveling: Moving a pivot foot or taking more than two steps without dribbling the ball.
  2. Double Dribble: Dribbling the ball with both hands simultaneously or stopping a dribble and then starting again.
  3. Carrying: Placing the hand underneath the ball while dribbling, allowing the player to pause or gain an advantage.
  4. Backcourt Violation: Moving the ball from the frontcourt back into the backcourt after having already crossed the half-court line.
  5. 3-Second Violation: An offensive player staying in the key (paint) for more than three consecutive seconds.
  6. 8-Second Violation: Failing to advance the ball past the half-court line within eight seconds.
  7. Goaltending: Touching the ball while it is on a downward flight toward the basket, on the rim, or in the cylinder above the rim.
  8. Out of Bounds: Causing the ball to touch the floor or an object outside the boundary lines.
  9. Kicked Ball: Intentionally striking the ball with the leg or foot.
